Fountain

1917, replica 1964, Marcel Duchamp

Medium: Porcelain urinal

Fountain is Duchamp’s most famous example of a “readymade” sculpture. It is an inverted male urinal, signed "R. Mutt." This work challenged traditional definitions of art, subverting conventional notions of art and beauty. Duchamp argued that the essence of art lies in ideas rather than the craftsmanship of the object. By selecting everyday objects and giving them new contexts and meanings, he emphasized the intellectual aspect of art, pioneering conceptual art and profoundly influencing the development of 20th-century art.
